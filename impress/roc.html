Lesson

CAS is a fundamental synchronization primitive for concurrent algorithms.

At a closer inspection,

CAS is two primitives at once.

An agreement primitive 


      ...
      val ch = createChild(x, k, v)
      if (!CAS(a(i), x, ch))
        insert(k, v, a, d)
      ...

All threads agree that the value changed from old to new.

A failure-detection primitive 


      ...
      val ch = createChild(x, k, v)
      if (!CAS(a(i), x, ch))
        insert(k, v, a, d)
      ...

The update failed, and the operation must be restarted.

Lock-Freedom

A concurrent program is lock-free if, after some finite number of steps, at least one of the threads completes an operation.
